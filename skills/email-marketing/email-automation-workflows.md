# Email Automation Workflows

Email automation workflows allow you to send the right message to the right person at the right time — without manually sending each email. This skill covers how to design, build, and optimize automated email sequences that drive engagement and conversions.

## What You'll Learn

- The core types of email automation workflows
- How to map workflows to the customer journey
- Best practices for trigger-based emails
- How to measure and improve workflow performance

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of email marketing platforms (Mailchimp, Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, etc.)
- Familiarity with audience segmentation
- Completed: Subject Line Optimization (recommended)

---

## Core Concepts

### What Is an Email Automation Workflow?

An automation workflow is a series of emails sent automatically based on a **trigger** — a specific action or condition that a subscriber meets. Examples of triggers:

- Signing up for a newsletter
- Abandoning a shopping cart
- Making a purchase
- Not opening emails for 90 days
- Clicking a specific link

### The Three Pillars of Effective Workflows

1. **Trigger** — What starts the sequence?
2. **Logic** — What conditions branch the path? (opened/didn't open, clicked/didn't click)
3. **Action** — What email (or other action) is sent?

## Essential Workflow Types

### 1. Welcome Series
Sent immediately after a subscriber joins your list. Goal: introduce your brand and set expectations.

**Recommended structure:**
- Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + deliver lead magnet if applicable
- Email 2 (Day 2): Your brand story / what makes you different
- Email 3 (Day 4): Most popular content or product
- Email 4 (Day 7): Soft call-to-action (trial, discount, consultation)

### 2. Abandoned Cart
Triggered when a user adds items to a cart but doesn't complete checkout.

**Recommended structure:**
- Email 1 (1 hour after abandonment): Friendly reminder
- Email 2 (24 hours): Address common objections (returns policy, reviews)
- Email 3 (72 hours): Urgency or limited-time incentive

### 3. Post-Purchase Onboarding
Helps new customers get value quickly and reduces buyer's remorse.

**Recommended structure:**
- Email 1: Order confirmation + what to expect
- Email 2: Getting started tips / how-to guide
- Email 3: Ask for a review or share on social
- Email 4: Cross-sell or upsell relevant products

### 4. Re-engagement (Win-Back) Campaign
Targets subscribers who haven't engaged in 60–90 days.

**Recommended structure:**
- Email 1: "We miss you" — highlight what's new
- Email 2: Offer an incentive to return
- Email 3: Final notice — ask if they want to stay subscribed
- If no engagement: suppress or remove from list

## Timing & Frequency Best Practices

| Workflow Type | First Email Delay | Typical Sequence Length |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome Series | Immediate | 4–7 emails over 2 weeks |
| Abandoned Cart | 30–60 minutes | 2–3 emails over 3 days |
| Post-Purchase | Immediate | 3–5 emails over 2 weeks |
| Re-engagement | N/A (scheduled) | 2–3 emails over 1 week |

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Open Rate** — Are subject lines compelling?
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** — Is the content relevant and the CTA clear?
- **Conversion Rate** — Are emails driving the desired action?
- **Unsubscribe Rate** — Are you emailing too frequently or irrelevantly?
- **Revenue per Email** — For e-commerce workflows, track direct revenue attribution

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Sending too many emails too fast** — Respect your subscriber's inbox
- **No branching logic** — Treat everyone the same regardless of behavior
- **Forgetting to test** — Always A/B test subject lines and send times
- **Never auditing old workflows** — Stale automations can hurt deliverability
- **Ignoring mobile optimization** — Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile

## Practice Exercise

Map out a welcome series for a fictional SaaS product:

2. Write subject lines for 4 emails
3. Identify one branch condition (e.g., user completed setup vs. didn't)
4. Define the goal metric for each email
