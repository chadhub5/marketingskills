# Keyword Research Fundamentals

Keyword research is the foundation of any successful SEO strategy. It helps you understand what your target audience is searching for and how to create content that matches their intent.

## What You'll Learn

- How to identify seed keywords for your niche
- Understanding search volume and keyword difficulty
- How to analyze search intent
- Tools and techniques for keyword discovery
- How to build a keyword list and prioritize opportunities

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of what SEO is
- Access to at least one keyword research tool (free options listed below)

## Core Concepts

### Search Intent

Every search query falls into one of four intent categories:

1. **Informational** — The user wants to learn something (e.g., "how to do keyword research")
2. **Navigational** — The user wants to find a specific site (e.g., "Google Search Console login")
3. **Commercial** — The user is researching before buying (e.g., "best SEO tools 2024")
4. **Transactional** — The user is ready to take action (e.g., "buy Ahrefs subscription")

Matching your content to the correct intent is more important than keyword volume alone.

### Key Metrics to Evaluate

| Metric | What It Tells You | Ideal Range (Beginners) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Search Volume | Monthly searches for the keyword | 100–5,000 |
| Keyword Difficulty (KD) | How hard it is to rank | 0–30 |
| Cost Per Click (CPC) | Commercial value of the keyword | Higher = more valuable |
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | % of searchers who click results | Varies by SERP features |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Brainstorm Seed Keywords

Start with 5–10 broad topics relevant to your business or content. For a marketing blog, these might be:
- email marketing
- social media strategy
- content marketing
- SEO basics
- paid advertising

### Step 2: Expand with a Keyword Tool

Plug your seed keywords into a tool to find related terms, questions, and long-tail variations.

**Free tools:**
- [Google Keyword Planner](https://ads.google.com/home/tools/keyword-planner/) — best for volume data
- [Ubersuggest](https://neilpatel.com/ubersuggest/) — good for beginners
- [AnswerThePublic](https://answerthepublic.com/) — great for question-based keywords
- [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console) — shows what you already rank for

**Paid tools:**
- Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz — more accurate data and competitor analysis

### Step 3: Filter and Prioritize

Use this scoring framework to prioritize your list:

```
Opportunity Score = (Search Volume × Relevance) / Keyword Difficulty
```

Focus on keywords where:
- Relevance to your audience is high (7–10/10)
- KD is below 30 if your site is new
- Search volume is enough to matter (100+ searches/month)

### Step 4: Group Keywords by Topic

Cluster related keywords together — these will become individual pages or sections of content. Avoid creating separate pages for keywords that share the same intent.

**Example cluster:**
- Primary: "keyword research for beginners"
- Supporting: "how to do keyword research", "keyword research tutorial", "keyword research steps"

### Step 5: Map Keywords to Content

Create a simple spreadsheet with columns:
- Keyword
- Search Volume
- KD
- Intent
- Assigned Page/Post
- Status (planned / in progress / published)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Targeting only high-volume keywords** — These are usually too competitive for new long-tail keywords** — They convert better and are easier to rank for
- **Keyword stuffing** — Write for humans first; use keywords naturally
- **Skipping competitor analysis** — Look at what's already ranking to understand the bar

## Practice Exercise

1. Pick one topic you want to create content about
2. Brainstorm 5 seed keywords
3. Use a free tool to find 20 related keywords
4. Filter down to your top 5 based on the criteria above
5. Identify the search intent for each

## Summary

Effective keyword research combines data (volume, difficulty) with human understanding (intent, relevance). Start small, focus on realistic opportunities, and build your keyword strategy iteratively as your site grows authority.
